These circles are drawn using trigonometry, which makes them more customisable, rather than turtle's built in Turtle.circle() method. The hypotemuse of the right angled triangle's length is the radius of the circle. The right angle can be at either 0,0 or x,y of the point. One of the is set to the angle in the circle that is being found out (first 0, then 1, then 2 etc.), and using the formula sin θ = opposite / hypotemuse, rearanged to opposite = sin θ * hypotemuse we work out the y, as the opposite will be a straight vertical line. We also use cos θ = adjacent / hypotemuse, rearranged to adjacent = cos θ * hypotemuse, to work out the x, as the adjacent side will be a straight horizontal line. Therefore, presuming the centre of the circle is at [0,0], the points x will be the length of the adjacent and the y that of the opposite.

Variation 1 shows these triangles in use, which then creates a pattern

In variations 2, 3, and 4, patterns appear because lines cannot be perfectly straight on a computer if they are diagonal and sometimes the lines are closer together or further apart; these patterns are not purposefully generated through code.
